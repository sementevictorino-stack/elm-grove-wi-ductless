Common Heating System Problems We Fix

Our technicians diagnose and repair all common heating system issues:

No Heat or Insufficient Heat

  • Thermostat Malfunctions: Faulty thermostats preventing system operation
  • Ignition Problems: Pilot light, igniter, or flame sensor issues
  • Gas Supply Issues: Gas valve, regulator, or supply line problems
  • Electrical Failures: Control board, wiring, or electrical component issues
  • Blocked Vents: Intake or exhaust vent blockages affecting operation
  • Safety Switch Activation: Safety switches preventing system operation

Intermittent Operation

  • Short Cycling: System turning on and off too frequently
  • Flame Sensor Issues: Dirty or faulty flame sensors causing shutdowns
  • Pressure Switch Problems: Pressure switches preventing proper operation
  • Heat Exchanger Issues: Cracked or damaged heat exchangers
  • Limit Switch Problems: Overheating protection switches activating

Unusual Noises

  • Banging or Popping: Ductwork expansion or ignition issues
  • Squealing or Grinding: Motor, bearing, or belt problems
  • Rattling: Loose components or cabinet issues
  • Rumbling: Combustion or gas valve problems
  • Clicking: Electrical relay or control issues

Air Quality and Airflow Issues

  • Poor Air Circulation: Blower motor or ductwork problems
  • Dirty Air: Filter, ductwork, or indoor air quality issues
  • Uneven Heating: Ductwork, damper, or system balance problems
  • Strange Odors: Combustion, electrical, or system cleanliness issues

Repair vs. Replacement Guidance

We provide honest advice on when to repair versus replace:

Repair Indicators

  • System Age: Units under 10 years typically worth repairing
  • Repair Cost: Repairs costing less than 50% of replacement value
  • Overall Condition: Well-maintained systems with isolated issues
  • Energy Efficiency: Systems still operating at acceptable efficiency levels

Replacement Indicators

  • Advanced Age: Systems over 15-20 years old
  • Frequent Repairs: Multiple repairs needed annually
  • High Energy Costs: Systems with declining efficiency
  • Major Component Failure: Heat exchanger or other costly component failures
  • Safety Concerns: Systems with safety issues or code violations
