What Constitutes an HVAC Emergency?

Understanding when to call for emergency service helps ensure your safety and prevents further damage to your system:

Immediate Emergency Situations

  • Complete System Failure in Extreme Weather: No heating during freezing temperatures or no cooling during dangerous heat
  • Gas Leaks or Odors: Any smell of natural gas or propane requires immediate professional attention
  • Electrical Issues: Sparks, burning smells, or electrical shorts in HVAC equipment
  • Carbon Monoxide Detection: CO detector alarms or symptoms of CO poisoning
  • Water Damage Risk: Excessive water leakage that could cause property damage
  • Safety Hazards: Any condition that poses immediate risk to occupants

Urgent Repair Situations

  • Significant Performance Loss: System running but providing inadequate heating or cooling
  • Strange Noises: Grinding, squealing, banging, or other unusual sounds
  • Refrigerant Leaks: Hissing sounds or ice buildup on ductless mini split systems
  • Thermostat Malfunctions: Complete loss of temperature control
  • Ductwork Issues: Major air leaks or disconnected ducts

Preventing HVAC Emergencies

While we're always ready to help in emergencies, prevention is the best strategy:

Regular Maintenance Programs

  • Seasonal Inspections: Pre-season checks to identify potential problems

Warning Signs to Watch For

  • Unusual Noises: New or changing sounds from your HVAC system
  • Performance Changes: Reduced heating or cooling capacity
  • Higher Energy Bills: Sudden increases in utility costs
  • Frequent Cycling: System turning on and off repeatedly
  • Air Quality Issues: Increased dust, humidity, or odors

Safety During HVAC Emergencies

Your safety is our top priority. Follow these guidelines during HVAC emergencies:

Gas-Related Emergencies

  • Evacuate Immediately: If you smell gas, leave the premises immediately
  • Don't Use Electrical Switches: Avoid creating sparks that could ignite gas
  • Call from Outside: Use a phone away from the building to call for help
  • Don't Re-Enter: Wait for professional clearance before returning

Electrical Emergencies

  • Turn Off Power: Switch off circuit breakers to affected equipment
  • Avoid Water: Keep electrical components dry
  • Don't Touch Equipment: Avoid contact with potentially energized components
  • Call Professionals: Electrical issues require professional diagnosis
