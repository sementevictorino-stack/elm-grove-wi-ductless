Common Indoor Air Quality Problems

Wisconsin homes face unique indoor air quality challenges:

Airborne Pollutants

  • Dust and Dust Mites: Accumulation of household dust and microscopic dust mites
  • Pet Dander: Allergens from cats, dogs, and other household pets
  • Pollen: Seasonal pollen that enters homes and circulates in HVAC systems
  • Mold Spores: Microscopic mold spores from moisture and humidity issues
  • Bacteria and Viruses: Airborne pathogens that circulate through home air systems
  • Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs): Chemical emissions from household products

Humidity Issues

  • High Humidity: Excess moisture leading to mold growth and discomfort
  • Low Humidity: Dry air causing respiratory irritation and static electricity
  • Seasonal Fluctuations: Dramatic humidity changes between seasons
  • Uneven Humidity: Inconsistent humidity levels throughout the home
  • Moisture Problems: Basement, crawl space, and bathroom moisture issues

Ventilation Problems

  • Poor Air Circulation: Inadequate air movement and stagnant air areas
  • Insufficient Fresh Air: Lack of outdoor air infiltration in tight homes
  • Contaminated Ductwork: Dirty or damaged ductwork spreading pollutants
  • Exhaust Issues: Poor bathroom and kitchen ventilation
  • Air Pressure Imbalances: Negative or positive pressure affecting air quality

Chemical and Odor Issues

  • Household Chemicals: Cleaning products, paints, and adhesives
  • Combustion Pollutants: Gases from fireplaces, stoves, and furnaces
  • Tobacco Smoke: Residual smoke and third-hand smoke contamination
  • Cooking Odors: Lingering food odors and cooking byproducts
  • Pet Odors: Animal-related odors throughout the home
