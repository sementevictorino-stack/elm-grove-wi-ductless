Common Ductless Mini Split Problems We Repair

Our expert technicians are trained to diagnose and repair a wide range of ductless mini split issues:

Refrigerant-Related Issues

  • Refrigerant Leaks: We locate and repair leaks in refrigerant lines, then recharge the system to proper levels
  • Low Refrigerant Levels: Proper refrigerant charging for optimal cooling and heating performance
  • Refrigerant Line Damage: Repair or replacement of damaged copper lines and insulation
  • Pressure Imbalances: Correction of high or low pressure conditions affecting system performance

Electrical Component Failures

  • Compressor Problems: Diagnosis and repair of compressor issues including overheating and electrical failures
  • Fan Motor Repairs: Indoor and outdoor fan motor replacement and repair services
  • Control Board Issues: Circuit board troubleshooting and replacement for both indoor and outdoor units
  • Sensor Malfunctions: Temperature and pressure sensor diagnosis and replacement
  • Wiring Problems: Electrical connection repair and code compliance restoration

Airflow and Performance Issues

  • Clogged Filters: Filter cleaning and replacement for optimal airflow
  • Blocked Coils: Indoor and outdoor coil cleaning to restore heat transfer efficiency
  • Drainage Problems: Condensate drain cleaning and repair to prevent water damage
  • Ductless Unit Mounting: Repositioning or reinforcing improperly mounted indoor units

Cold Weather Performance Issues

  • Ice Buildup: Defrost cycle repairs and ice prevention solutions
  • Heating Mode Failures: Heat pump reversing valve repair and replacement
  • Cold Climate Adaptations: Modifications for improved Wisconsin winter performance
  • Frost Protection: Installation of cold weather kits and protection systems

Emergency Repair Scenarios

Certain situations require immediate professional attention to prevent further damage or safety hazards:

Immediate Emergency Situations

  • Complete System Failure: No heating or cooling during extreme weather
  • Electrical Issues: Sparks, burning smells, or electrical shorts
  • Refrigerant Leaks: Hissing sounds or chemical odors
  • Water Damage: Excessive condensation or drainage failures
  • Strange Noises: Grinding, squealing, or banging sounds

Urgent Repair Needs

  • Reduced Performance: Insufficient heating or cooling capacity
  • High Energy Bills: Sudden increases in energy consumption
  • Frequent Cycling: System turning on and off repeatedly
  • Uneven Temperatures: Hot or cold spots throughout your home
  • Poor Air Quality: Musty odors or increased dust levels

Repair vs. Replacement Decision Making

Sometimes repairs aren't the most cost-effective solution. Our honest assessment helps you make informed decisions:

When to Repair

  • System is less than 8 years old
  • Repair costs are less than 50% of replacement cost
  • Major components (compressor, heat exchangers) are functioning properly
  • System has been well-maintained with regular service
  • Current efficiency meets your comfort needs

When to Consider Replacement

  • System is over 12 years old with multiple component failures
  • Repair costs exceed 60% of replacement cost
  • Frequent repairs have become a pattern
  • Energy efficiency is significantly below current standards
  • Refrigerant type is being phased out

Troubleshooting Tips for Homeowners

Before calling for professional repair, try these simple troubleshooting steps:

Basic Checks

  • Verify the unit is receiving power and circuit breakers are on
  • Check that the remote control has fresh batteries
  • Ensure the indoor unit air filter is clean
  • Confirm the outdoor unit isn't blocked by snow, leaves, or debris
  • Check that all vents and returns are open and unobstructed

When to Call Professionals

  • System won't turn on despite power being available
  • Strange noises, smells, or visible damage
  • System runs but doesn't heat or cool effectively
  • Ice buildup on indoor or outdoor units
  • Water leaking from indoor units
  • Electrical issues or sparking
